# Residual Analysis

## Purpose

Residual analysis was performed to evaluate the predictive performance of the Multiple Regression model and identify observations where actual sales differed substantially from predicted sales.

## Largest Positive Residuals

Positive residuals indicate stores where actual sales exceeded model predictions.

### Interpretation

The observations with the largest positive residuals represent stores that performed significantly better than expected. These stores may have benefited from factors not included in the model, such as local promotions, seasonal demand, superior customer service, or favorable market conditions.

The presence of positive residuals suggests opportunities for further investigation into high-performing stores.

---

## Largest Negative Residuals

Negative residuals indicate stores where actual sales were lower than predicted.

### Interpretation

The observations with the largest negative residuals represent stores that underperformed relative to model expectations. Potential reasons may include operational issues, inventory shortages, local competition, or temporary disruptions that are not captured by the available variables.

These stores should be examined further to identify causes of underperformance.

---

## Overall Assessment

Residuals were observed on both sides of zero, indicating that the model does not consistently overestimate or underestimate Monthly Sales.

The residual distribution suggests that the Multiple Regression model provides a reasonably accurate representation of the relationship between the selected variables and Monthly Sales.

## Conclusion

The residual analysis supports the validity of the Multiple Regression model and indicates that it can be used as a reliable tool for business insight generation and sales prediction.
