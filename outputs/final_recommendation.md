# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

The regression analysis identified Footfall and Marketing Spend as the strongest drivers of Monthly Sales. Regional differences were also found to influence store performance, particularly in the South and West regions.

## Key Findings

### Footfall

Footfall emerged as the strongest individual predictor of Monthly Sales. Stores with higher customer traffic consistently achieved stronger sales performance.

### Marketing Spend

Marketing Spend demonstrated a positive and statistically significant relationship with Monthly Sales. Strategic marketing investments can contribute to improved sales outcomes.

### Regional Effects

Stores located in the South and West regions displayed significantly higher sales performance compared with the reference region.

## Recommended Actions

### Increase Customer Traffic

Management should prioritize initiatives that increase store visits, including promotional campaigns, customer engagement activities, and improved customer experience.

### Optimize Marketing Investments

Marketing budgets should be allocated toward stores and regions with strong growth potential and high customer responsiveness.

### Focus on High-Performing Regions

The South and West regions should receive additional strategic attention due to their positive impact on sales performance.

### Monitor Underperforming Stores

Stores associated with large negative residuals should be investigated to identify operational challenges and improvement opportunities.

## Limitations

* The model explains approximately 79.3% of sales variation but does not capture every factor affecting performance.
* External market conditions and economic factors were not included in the dataset.
* Regression analysis identifies associations rather than causal relationships.

## Conclusion

The Multiple Regression model provides the best representation of the factors influencing Monthly Sales. The findings indicate that increasing Footfall, optimizing Marketing Spend, and leveraging regional strengths can support improved business performance and more effective decision-making.
